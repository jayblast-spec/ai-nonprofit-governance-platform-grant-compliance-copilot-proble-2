## Product

AI Nonprofit Governance Platform

## One Sentence

Streamline grant compliance by extracting obligations from agreements and tracking reporting deadlines.

## Personas

- teams working on agreements and compliance

## Pains

- People need a clearer way to act on ai-nonprofit-governance-platform-grant-compliance-copilot. Problem: Grant obligations are buried in PDFs and emails, causing missed reporting dates and weak evidence trails. Target user: grant managers, nonprofit operations teams, and foundation reporting staff Implementation type: document intake and compliance tracker with obligation extraction, calendar reminders, and evidence collection Core features: grant PDF intake, obligation extraction, reporting calendar, evidence checklist, funder-ready status report Difficulty: high Monetization potential: very high Strategic value: Turns compliance anxiety into a recurring operational system nonprofits will pay to keep current. Memory context available; reuse similar ArkNet patterns where they fit. First milestone: Extract obligations from one grant agreement and create a 90-day reporting checklist. Positioning: AI Nonprofit Governance Platform should win grant managers, nonprofit operations teams, and foundation reporting staff by turning grant agreements, funder emails, and reporting PDFs into a dated obligation calendar, evidence checklist, and funder-ready status report, with source citations, reporting windows, evidence owners, funder names, and confidence labels visible before anyone exports or shares it. First proof: Extract obligations from one grant agreement and create a 90-day reporting checklist. Pricing model: Free first grant import, $79/month Grant Desk for one program, $249/month Compliance Team with reporting calendars/evidence packs, custom annual plan for multi-funder portfolios. Moat: The moat is funder-obligation memory: every grant PDF, reporting date, evidence artifact, and completed checklist improves future compliance templates for that nonprofit. Validation test: Run a paid-intent proof with 5 target users: watch them complete "Extract obligations from one grant agreement and create a 90-day reporting checklist.", measure time-to-value under 2 minutes, then ask whether they would pay for saved history, exports, alerts, or team access. Landing page strategy: Hero: grant compliance outcome with obligation extraction, reporting calendar, evidence checklist, and funder-ready status.; Problem proof: dramatize the gap behind "Grant obligations are buried in PDFs and emails, causing missed reporting dates and weak evidence trails." with meeting notes, policies, grants, or donor evidence scattered across tools.; Product section: grant PDF intake, obligation extraction, 90-day reporting calendar, evidence checklist, and funder-ready export.; Trust: source citations, reporting windows, evidence owners, funder names, and confidence labels, role permissions, audit history, and human review before final output.; CTA: import a grant agreement and generate the first 90-day compliance checklist. Inputs needed: grant agreement PDF, funder reporting dates, required evidence list, program owner, finance owner, reporting format, renewal deadline Trust assets: source citations, obligation confidence, funder-ready export, owner/date audit trail, evidence checklist, privacy statement.

evidence-backed governance ledger that extracts decisions, owners, due dates, policy links, and board-ready packets
